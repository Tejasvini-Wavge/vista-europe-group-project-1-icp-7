Rome the Eternal City

Rome, Italy

Rome, the capital city of Italy, is an exhilarating blend of historical marvels, vibrant cultural life, and culinary delights. Known as the "Eternal City," Rome's history spans over 28 centuries, making it one of the oldest continuously occupied sites in Europe. From its ancient origins to its role as the heart of the Roman Empire and its significance in the development of the Catholic Church, Rome is a treasure trove of architectural and artistic masterpieces.

Historical and Cultural Highlights

The Colosseum

An iconic symbol of ancient Rome, the Colosseum is an amphitheater that hosted gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. It remains a powerful testament to Roman architectural and engineering prowess.

The Roman Forum

Once the center of Roman public life, the Forum houses the ruins of several important ancient government buildings. Walking through the Forum is like stepping back in time.

Vatican City

An independent city-state enclaved within Rome, it is the spiritual and administrative center of the Roman Catholic Church. St. Peter's Basilica, the Vatican Museums, and the Sistine Chapel, with Michelangelo's ceiling, are must-visit sites.
