Cities rich in Heritage and Culture

Rome, Italy

Known as the "Eternal City," Rome is a living museum with ancient ruins like the Colosseum and Roman Forum, alongside the Vatican City, home to the Pope and a treasure trove of art and history.

Athens, Greece

The cradle of Western civilization, Athens is home to ancient landmarks such as the Acropolis, the Parthenon, and the Ancient Agora, offering a glimpse into ancient Greek life.

Istanbul, Turkey

Straddling two continents, Istanbul's rich history is visible in its Byzantine and Ottoman architecture, including the Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, and the Topkapi Palace.

London, England

London's history stretches back to Roman times, with highlights including the Tower of London, the British Museum, and Westminster Abbey, alongside a dynamic cultural scene.

Granada, Spain

Famous for the Alhambra, a stunning example of Moorish art, Granada reflects the rich cultural fusion of its Islamic, Christian, and Jewish heritage.

St. Petersburg, Russia

Founded by Tsar Peter the Great, St. Petersburg is known for the Hermitage Museum, the Winter Palace, and the Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood.
