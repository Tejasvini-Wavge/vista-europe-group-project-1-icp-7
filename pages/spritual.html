SPIRITUAL AND RELIGIOUS
TOURISM

If you would like to go on a spiritual journey, Italy is the right country.
Sanctuaries in Italy are places devoted to
their own spirituality.

The Pontifical Sanctuary of
the Holy Stairs

The Pontifical Sanctuary of the Holy Stairs in Rome holds historical significance, believed to be the stairs Jesus ascended during his trial.

The Sanctuary of Monte
Grisa in Trieste

The Sanctuary of Monte Grisa in Trieste, Italy, is a notable pilgrimage site offering panoramic views of the city and the Adriatic Sea.

The Sanctuary of
Padre Pio

The city where Padre Pio da Pietrelcina lived and worked celebrates him with the splendid Sanctuary designed by Renzo Piano

Shrine of the Holy House
of Loreto

The Shrine of the Holy House of Loreto, situated in Loreto, Italy, is revered as the house where the Virgin Mary received the Annunciation.

Sanctuary of the Seven Churches
in Monselice

The Sanctuary of the Seven Churches, or the Jubilee Sanctuary of the Seven Churches, is located in Monselice in the province of Padua.

Via Francigena

Once an ancient pilgrimage route to Rome and the Holy Land in the name of inner awareness.The Via Francigena is an ancient pilgrimage route that spans over 1,200 miles.

ITALY

Italy, situated in Southern Europe, is a captivating blend of rich history, cultural splendor, and breathtaking landscapes. The country, renowned for its iconic boot-shaped peninsula, boasts a population of around 60 million. With Rome as its capital, Italy is a parliamentary representative democratic republic, known for its influential contributions to art, literature, and science throughout history. The Italian cuisine, celebrated globally, features delectable dishes like pasta and pizza. Tourists flock to Italy to explore its historic landmarks, including the Colosseum, the Vatican City, and the Leaning Tower of Pisa.
